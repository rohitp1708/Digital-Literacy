# Case Study (Phishing Attack):

Riya, a college student in India, received an email that
appeared to be from her bank. The email warned her that her
account would be blocked unless she verified her details
immediately. It included a link that looked official. Trusting
the message, Riya clicked the link and was taken to a fake
website that closely resembled her bank’s login page. She
entered her username, password, and OTP.

Within minutes, the attacker used this information to access

her real bank account. They transferred money through UPI
transactions to multiple accounts, making it difficult to trace.
Riya only realized something was wrong when she received
debit alerts for transactions she had not made.
Phishing attacks like this typically involve creating urgency
and fear to trick victims into sharing sensitive information.
Attackers often target students, elderly individuals, or anyone
unfamiliar with cybersecurity practices. The consequences can
include financial loss, identity theft, and emotional stress. In
some cases, victims may also face difficulty recovering funds
or securing their accounts.
This case highlights the importance of verifying email sources,
avoiding suspicious links, and never sharing confidential
information like OTPs or passwords online.
